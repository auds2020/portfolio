Whale Migraton Visualizer

  • CategoryFor fun
  • Project dateMarch 2026 (weekend hackathon)
  • Skills usedAdobe Illustrator, laser cutting, CAD

Motivation

Whales have some of the longest migrations of any mammal on Earth. During these journeys, whales fertilize the marine ecosystems they move through and support the marine life inhabiting them. However, many of these migrations fall in the paths of major shipping highways across the Atlantic and Pacific.

Our team, comprised of myself and 5 Master's students at the MIT Sea Grant, wanted to visualize whale migration patterns to visually communicate the diversity and spread of migration patterns around the world. We show eight main species of whales, each represented by a separate color of LED lights, and represent the migration patterns in two forms. One press of the button shows the current time on our LED panel and lights up where the whales currently are for each species. Click the button again, and you see a time-lapse mode of a full year of the migration patterns - perhaps a more visually appealing format.

Mechanics (my focus)

I was on the mechanical half of the team. We first started in Onshape, an online, collaborative CAD software, where we planned out each layer and how the layers would connect. We then used a laser cutter to cut out each layer as well as all the supports on 1/8th in plywood, and then glued everything together with wood glue. I made the wood frame out of plywood on the table saw, with 1/8th in grooves for the bottom two layers. We used iron-on maple veneer to hide the ugly plywood layers. We painted each layer of wood to represent a different area of the ocean.

Electronics

On the electronics side, a state machine was set up with two buttons to control the four main modes of the system.

  • Current time mode, where the LED that depicts the current location of the species of whales lights up.
  • A time-lapse of the entire year's worth of migrations in 30 seconds
  • Rainbow mode, where we cycle through the whole rainbow
  • OFF mode, the LED strip turns off, and a stationary whale is displayed on the LED panel
