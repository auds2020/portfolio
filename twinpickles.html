About this project

For 2.017, Design of Electromechanical Robotic Systems, we were asked to build something which would help researchers study harmful algal blooms. After consulting with experts and discussing our learning objectives, we evaluated that one useful niche which we would be able to assist in is soil sampling.

Some types of harmful algal blooms, including some causing paralytic shellfish poisoning, have a particular stage of their lifecycle called cysts, where the algae goes dormant to protect itself until more favorable conditions arise. However, what causes cysts to bloom is poorly understood as collecting samples is difficult and expensive. We wanted to create a boat which could autonomously collect soil samples to help overcome this bottleneck.

I assisted in the mechanical design of the catamaran platform, working closely with the sampling team to ensure integration. In our final test in the Charles River, we demonsted RC control of our vehicle, and remote raising and lowering of our sampler.

To learn more about the entire system overall, as well as our overall design process, please read our final presentation and our final report.
